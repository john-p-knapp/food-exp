# Chicken Soup with Dumplings 

* From Thomas Keller, Ad Hoc at Home. Serves 6 (about 8 cups). 
### Ingredients: 
* 8 tbl butter
* 1/2 cup flour
* 3 tbl flour
* 1/2 cup water
* 4 tbl butter
* 1.5 tsp salt
* 2/3 cup flour
* 1 tsp dijon mustard
* 2 eggs
* 4 tsp chives
* 1 tbl butter
* 1 cup carrots
* 1 cup celery
* 1 cup onion
* 1 cup leeks
*  salt
* 1 gallon chicken stock
* 5 stalk celery
* 3 carrots
* 1 tsp honey
* 1 bay leaf
* 2 sprig thyme
* 1 cloves garlic
* 2 cup chicken
* 1/4 cup chives
* 1 tbl champagne vinegar
*  pepper
*  flat-leaf parsley

### Directions: 

#### Roux {
1. Melt **8 tbl unsalted butter** in a small saucepan over medium heat. When it is almost melted, whisk in **1/2 cup all-purpose flour** and **3 tbl all-purpose flour** and cook, whisking constantly and adjusting the heat so the roux bubbles but does not brown, 3 to 4 minutes. 
2. Transfer to a container to cool, then refrigerate up to 2 weeks. Makes about 2/3 cup. 

}


#### Dumplings {
1. Combine **1/2 cup water** , **4 tbl unsalted butter** , and **1.5 tsp kosher salt** in a medium saucepan and bring to a simmer over medium-high heat. 
2. Reduce to medium, add **2/3 cup all-purpose flour** all at once, and stir rapidly until the dough pulls away from the sides and the bottom of the pan is clean, glossy, and smooth but still moist, 4 to 5 minutes. 
3. Transfer the dough to a stand mixer fitted with the paddle. Add **1 tsp dijon mustard** and mix briefly, then on the lowest speed add **2 large eggs** one at a time, fully incorporating each before the next, then mix in **4 tsp minced chives** . 
4. Using two soupspoons, shape the dough into quenelles and drop them into simmering salted water, cooking in batches of about 6. Once they rise, cook about 5 minutes, then transfer with a slotted spoon to a parchment-lined baking sheet. Makes about 18. 

}

1. Melt **1 tbl unsalted butter** in an 8 to 10 quart stockpot over medium heat. Add **1 cup thinly sliced carrots** , **1 cup coarsely chopped celery** , **1 cup coarsely chopped onion** , and **1 cup coarsely chopped leeks** , season with **salt** , cover with a parchment lid, and cook very slowly over low heat, stirring occasionally, 30 to 35 minutes, until the vegetables are tender. 
2. Add **1 gallon chicken stock** to the vegetables and bring to a simmer. Simmer 30 minutes, then strain the soup base into another pot and discard the vegetables. 
3. Peel **5 stalk celery** and slice crosswise on the diagonal into thin pieces about 1 1 /2 inches long; you need about 1 1 /2 cups. Cook in a large pot of boiling salted water until just tender, then drain, cool in an ice bath, and drain again. 
4. Cut **3 large carrots** lengthwise into quarters and then crosswise into bite-sized pieces; you need about 1 1 /2 cups. Put the carrots in a saucepan, cover with cold water, and combine:  
**1 tsp honey**   
**1 bay leaf**   
**2 sprig thyme**   
**1 cloves crushed garlic**   

with a pinch of salt and pepper. 
5. Bring to a simmer and cook 4 to 5 minutes, until the carrots are tender but slightly resistant to the tooth. Drain and transfer to paper towels. 
6. Bring the soup base to a simmer and whisk in **1/2 cup Roux** a little at a time until thick enough to coat the back of a spoon. Simmer 30 minutes, skimming often. 
7. Add **Dumplings** , **2 cup cooked shredded chicken** , the carrots, the celery, and **1/4 cup minced chives** to the soup and heat through. 
8. Season with **1 tbl champagne vinegar** and salt and **pepper** to taste. Transfer to a large serving bowl and sprinkle with **flat-leaf parsley** .
