# Seared Pork Tenderloin with Cider Pan Sauce 

### Ingredients: 
* 1 tbl worcestershire sauce
* 1/2 tsp garlic
* 1 tbl olive oil
* 1 tbl coarse dijon mustard
* 2 tsp thyme
* 1/2 tsp pepper
* 2.5 lbs pork tenderloin
* 2 tsp salt
* 1/2 tsp pepper
* 1 tbl butter
* 2 tbl shallot
* 2 allspice
* 2 whole clove
* 2 juniper berries
* 1 tsp thyme
* 1/4 tsp black pepper
* 3 tbl apple cider vinegar
* 1 cup apple cider
* 1 tbl coarse dijon mustard
* 1/2 tsp salt
* 1 tbl chives
* 2 tbl butter

### Directions: 
1. Preheat the oven to 350F. Line a large rimmed baking sheet with aluminum foil; set aside. 

#### Glaze {
2. In a small bowl combine:  
**1 tbl worcestershire sauce**   
**1/2 tsp grated garlic**   
**1 tbl olive oil**   
**1 tbl coarse dijon mustard**   
**2 tsp finely chopped thyme**   
**1/2 tsp pepper**   


3. Whisk until smooth. 

}

1. Trim **2.5 lbs pork tenderloin** . Pat and season with **2 tsp salt** and **1/2 tsp pepper** ; set aside. 
2. Sear pork on all sides until well browned. Transfer to baking sheet. Do not wipe skillet. 
3. Brush with pork with **glaze** and bake util rosy pink (135F). 
4. While the pork bakes, melt **1 tbl butter** in the reserved skillet over medium-low. Add **2 tbl finely chopped shallot** , **2 allspice** , **2 whole clove** , **2 juniper berries** (if using), **1 tsp thyme** and **1/4 tsp black pepper** . Cook, stirring constantly, until the shallot is softened, about 1 minute. 
5. Add **3 tbl apple cider vinegar** and cook, scraping the bottom of the skillet to release any browned bits, until nearly evaporated. Add **1 cup apple cider** , **1 tbl coarse dijon mustard** and **1/2 tsp salt** . 
6. Bring to a simmer over medium-high and cook, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens and reduces to roughly a third of its original volume, about 8 minutes. Remove from the heat; discard the whole spices. Vigorously stir in **1 tbl finely chopped chives** and **2 tbl cold butter** until the sauce is glossy and emulsified, about 1 minute. 
7. Transfer the pork to a cutting board and cut into 1/2-inch-thick slices. Transfer to a platter and spoon the cider sauce over top. Garnish with additional chives.
